Introduction

In this experiment, we investigated how red cabbage juice acts as a pH indicator, changing color when mixed with various household substances.
Red cabbage contains anthocyanin, a natural pigment that reacts differently depending on the acidity or alkalinity (basicity) of the substance it
contacts.

Hypothesis

If we mix red cabbage juice with different household substances, then the juice will change color according to the pH level of each substance
— turning red/pink in acids, purple in neutrals, and green/yellow/blue in bases.
